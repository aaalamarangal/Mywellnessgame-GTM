# Demo Commenting
Parent: 3.Recognising fulfilling demand | CLAUDE.md

## What demo commenting is

Demo commenting is 1:1 messaging to individual founders based on their LinkedIn signals.
It is part of Value Creation OS — the fulfilling layer.

It is NOT the same as demo posting (which is Offer Creation OS).
Demo commenting is private and personal. Demo posting is public.

Both lead to the same place: a demo call.

---

## The one rule

Demo commenting is always a filter. Never a pitch.

**Filter** = names what is breaking for this founder (invites self-recognition)
**Push** = explains what mywellnessgame does (attracts everyone, including the wrong people)

The moment you explain the offer, you stop filtering.
You will get curiosity instead of fit.

---

## The three jobs

Demo commenting has three jobs to complete before a demo call is worth scheduling.

| Job | What it means | What the founder says |
|---|---|---|
| Job 1 — Gap named | Founder diagnosed themselves. They named what their current system can't do. | A specific gap, not just curiosity |
| Job 2 — Use case connected | Founder tied the gap to their specific situation right now. | A real situation, not "yeah I struggle sometimes" |
| Job 3 — Fit question asked | Founder shifted from "is this relevant?" to "how would this work for me?" | An implementation question about their workflow or which game to start with |

---

## What Job 1 looks like per founder type

Knowing the founder type tells you what kind of gap to listen for.

| Founder type | Gap they name at Job 1 | What they don't say but mean |
|---|---|---|
| Founder A (Burnout) | Something feels urgent and I can't name it | "I'm in acute stress and I don't have the concept to resolve it" |
| Founder B (Procrastination) | I know what I need to do but I'm not doing it | "I can't hold to the one thing when the day pulls me off it" |
| Founder C (Inconsistency) | I show up but I can't see if it's working | "My effort isn't compounding and I don't know why" |

The gap the founder names tells you which founder type you're dealing with.
Use this to go deeper in your next message — not to explain the offer.

---

## What to do at each stage

**After Job 1 (gap named):**
Send one message. Go deeper into the specific situation. Do not explain the offer.

**After Job 2 (use case connected):**
Send one message. Surface the fit question. Let them ask it — do not offer it.

**After Job 3 (fit question asked):**
Schedule the demo call. Start at the gap the founder already named.
See: 3.Value delivery- remembering demand/1.Remembering finding demand/1.context/demo-call.md

---

## When a founder defends instead of engaging

If you name a gap and the founder responds with a defense ("I already have a system" / "that doesn't apply to me"):

This is the defend-and-deflect pattern — a sub-pattern of Founder B (Procrastination).
They know the gap. The defense is protecting them from having to own the action.

What to do:
- Do not push back on the defense
- Do not explain the offer
- Go one layer deeper into their specific situation: "What does your system do when the day pulls you off the one thing?"
- Let the specificity break the defense — not your counter-argument

The defense is the last move before acknowledgment. Stay patient.
