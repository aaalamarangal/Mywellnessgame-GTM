# LinkedIn Signal Scraping
Parent: CLAUDE.md | Recognising capturing demand

## Purpose
This file documents how to find founder signals on LinkedIn.
Not all signal sources are equal. Replies are more honest than posts.

---

## Three Signal Sources

### Source 1 — Founder's own posts
What they publish publicly on their feed.
More performative — tends toward resolved insight, lessons learned, and thought leadership.
Useful for understanding the founder's philosophy and how they present themselves.
Watch for: polished wisdom that hints at an unresolved gap underneath.

### Source 2 — Founder's replies on other people's posts
What they comment on someone else's content.
Less performative — more honest about what's unresolved, what resonates, what they're still working through.
This is where blocked language surfaces most clearly.

**Key insight:** The strongest signal from Christina Qi came not from her own post but from a reply she left on someone else's comment:
> "I wasn't there for some of my friends' biggest days (graduations, weddings), and that still haunts me."

Her own post had resolved philosophy. Her reply had the real cost.

### Source 3 — Founder's replies to their own readers' comments
When a reader comments on the founder's post and the founder replies back.
The founder is responding in real time, often more candid than the original post.
Watch for: moments where the founder drops the polished voice and speaks directly.

---

## What to Look For

**Blocked language** (Rob Snyder):
- Expressing absence, wishing for something that isn't there
- "Wish this was my usual state"
- "I have no system for knowing"
- "still haunts me"

**Coping language** (Rob Snyder):
- Sharing resolved insight as formed philosophy
- Teaching what worked for them
- Reflecting on a lesson learned
- Meet coping founders at their level — don't position them as lacking

---

## How to Scan

1. Open the founder's LinkedIn profile
2. Read their recent posts (last 30 days)
3. Click into the comments on their posts — read their replies to readers
4. Search their comment activity on other people's posts
5. The signal is in the gap between what they present and what they reveal
