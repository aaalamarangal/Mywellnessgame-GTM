# Founder Post Sources

How I find founder posts that lead to a signal worth messaging on.

Related context: ../1.context/message-contexts/linkedin-signal-scraping.md

---

## Method 1: Algorithm Feed → Productivity Conversation → Profile Check

### Step 1: Let the LinkedIn algorithm work
Scroll the LinkedIn home feed. Do not search. Do not filter.
The algorithm surfaces posts from connections and followed accounts.

### Step 2: Spot a productivity or regulation conversation
A founder is already writing or commenting about how they work.
This is not about big business events. It is about their personal operating layer.
Look for:
- Posts about focus, energy, consistency, or overwhelm
- Comments on other people's posts about productivity tools or systems
- Reactions to how others manage their time or decisions
- Any signal that shows they are thinking about how they run themselves

This matters because it tells you the founder is already in that conversation.
You are not introducing a new topic. You are joining one already open.

### Step 3: Check their profile for more personal productivity posts
Go to their profile. Read their recent posts and activity.
Look for any post where they write about how they work, think, plan, or manage themselves.
This confirms the conversation is real and not a one-off comment.

### Step 4: The intersection is the signal
Founder is in a productivity or regulation conversation (they are thinking about how they work) + their profile has personal productivity posts (this is a recurring theme for them) = a signal worth reading through the game framework.

If the profile shows no personal productivity posts, the conversation was a one-off. Move on.

---

## Why this method works

The algorithm does the initial filter. It shows you people you are already loosely connected to.
The productivity conversation tells you the founder is in that mental space right now.
The profile check confirms this is not a one-off — it is a pattern.
Together they point to a specific gap — which is where the message starts.

## Method 2: Influencer / Complementary Content

Follow influencers talking about GTM, leadership, or founder psychology (e.g. Rob Snyder).
Go to their posts and find founders commenting there — more honest than posts on their own profile.
Check that founder's profile for personal productivity posts.

Why this works: comments on someone else's post reveal real reactions. Less performative than a founder's own posts.

---

## Method 3: Events / Live Demonstrations

Find events like Magic Festival where founders are presenting or demoing.
Check their profile for personal productivity posts.

Why this works: founders in motion at events are actively doing and building. The signal is current and specific.

---

## Method 4: Inbound through your own posts and comments

Shiva posts and comments publicly. Content does the work.
Founders who resonate DM him directly.
The conversation reveals the signal.

Why this works: inbound means the founder already raised their hand. The signal is self-declared.

---

## What this will eventually become (AI-native software)

The software will need to:
1. Monitor the LinkedIn feed and surface founders in productivity or regulation conversations (Method 1)
2. Monitor influencer posts and surface founders commenting there (Method 2)
3. Track events and surface founders presenting or demoing (Method 3)
4. Flag inbound DMs and surface the signal from the conversation (Method 4)
5. For all methods: check the founder's profile for personal productivity posts to confirm the signal is real
