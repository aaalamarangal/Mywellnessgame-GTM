## Clarity — How it feels
*(Context Debt — absence of qualities and/or concepts)*

**Definition:** The founder can't name what's wrong because they don't have the concept to name it with. The problem feels like the enemy — themselves, others, or the environment.

**Shiva's lived experience:**
"I was not aligning my astrological framework into my daily experiences. Once I did — the debt released. The concept was always available. I just wasn't applying it to what I was living."

**What this means for founders:**
Clarity debt feels like the problem is external. Resolution comes when the right concept meets the right experience.

**From the field:**
> "Most of what feels hard hasn't actually been about the stage of the company, or even the problem we're facing. The bigger factor has been how I relate to the problem... it comes from the story we attach to it."
— Thenuka Karunaratne

> "That groundedness isn't a distraction from success, but rather a prerequisite for it."
— Christina Qi

> "the founders who struggle most are usually stuck on one setting → either performing certainty they don't have, or over-sharing doubt without a comeback."
— Leta Lista

"I love supporting people to regulate themselves — and productivity can be defined by the individual."

Self-regulation is the foundation.
