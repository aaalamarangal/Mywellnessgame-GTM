## Recurring Patterns Observed

These patterns come from delivery-log.md entries and Remember - Work assessments.
Update this section as new patterns emerge.

**1. Regulation breaks execution**
Sujith knows what to do. The constraint is not capability — it is regulation.
Triggers: early mornings, nights, physical events, smoking weed first thing, sleeping late (after 11PM).
When dysregulated — he does not time block, does not honour time blocks, and defaults to being reactive.
His own words: "I keep on missing low friction pathways — just because I am not regulated."

**2. Tasks done but not from first principles**
Sujith delegates to interns without doing the work himself first.
He sent interns to find founder signals without first checking 3–4 accounts manually himself.
Result: interns couldn't qualify posts because Sujith hadn't built the qualification criteria from direct observation.
His own words: "I should have checked 3–4 founder accounts manually and found out signals relevant for my value creation capability."

**3. Concepts not yet internalised**
The messaging framework exists (Alek's concepts, M Framework from Shiva) but Sujith hasn't collated them into a working document.
He is trying to message founders without having the atomic-level concepts in place.
His own words: "Still my messaging is adhoc and not become scientific. I need to bring it to workflow level."

**4. Time blocking not happening**
High-leverage outcomes are identified but not time-blocked on calendar.
Without time blocks — interns calls and events consume the day.
His own words: "I need to be careful on 1/ time blocking on calendar 2/ honouring the time blocks."

**5. Pressure is a privilege — not yet owned**
Sujith recognises that Shiva and others putting pressure on him are signals to act immediately.
He is beginning to own this but it is not yet a reflex.
His own words: "Whenever they say something — that's a signal for me to stop everything else and do that immediately."

## What to Watch For in Sessions

- Is he describing events or naming learning? Push for learning, not description.
- Is dysregulation present in the language? If yes — start there before moving to tasks.
- Are next steps specific or generic? Generic = the constraint is not yet clear.
- Is the nano game connected to the macro? If it has drifted — name it.
- Is a concept missing that is blocking execution? That is a Clarity constraint, not an Action constraint — address at the concept level first.
