# Business Types

## The Rule
The 3 games (Macro, Micro, Nano) are the same for all businesses.
What differs is the business itself — what stage it is at and how it shows up within each game.

---

## The 3 Business Types

| | Business A | Business B | Business C |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stage** | 0-1 customers | 1-10 customers | 10+ customers |
| **Problem** | Breadth without focus | Length without consistency | Depth without visibility |
| **Symptoms** | Multiple ideas, no clear priority, scattered GTM | Wins feel random, can't replicate, inconsistent execution | Same problems recurring, patterns invisible, growth feels fragile |
| **Needs** | Clarity | Action | Lesson |
| **Game** | Macro | Micro | Nano |
| **Stress type** | Acute | Neither acute nor chronic | Chronic |
| **Debt type** | Context Debt | Content Debt | Formula Debt |
| **Tense** | Present | Future | Past |

---

## Business A

**Stage:** 0-1 customers
**Problem:** Breadth without focus
**Symptoms:**
- Multiple offers or ideas being tested simultaneously
- No repeatable process yet
- GTM feels scattered
- No clear priority

**Needs:** Clarity — focus on what matters first

---

## Business B

**Stage:** 1-10 customers
**Problem:** Length without consistency
**Symptoms:**
- Wins feel random and hard to explain
- Can't replicate what worked
- Execution is inconsistent
- Early traction exists but no system behind it

**Needs:** Action — a repeatable process to build on early traction

---

## Business C

**Stage:** 10+ customers
**Problem:** Depth without visibility
**Symptoms:**
- Team growing but patterns invisible
- Same problems recurring at scale
- Growth feels fragile
- Things are working but founder doesn't know why

**Needs:** Lesson — making the invisible patterns visible
