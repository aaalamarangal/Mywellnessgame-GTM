## Post 1 — March 2026

**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7432100526374424576/

**Post text:**
I've been listening to founders this week.

No pitch. No deck. No "quick 15 minutes" calendar link.

Just questions.

And honestly? The replies were more honest than I expected.

One founder said: "Wish this was my usual state" — after reading a post about staying calm. (Relatable. Same.)

Another said: "The most valuable knowledge can't be downloaded." — Turns out Google doesn't have all the answers. Who knew.

A third said: "Self-management is your operating system." — Before leading a team, lead yourself. Apparently this is harder than it sounds.

Three founders. Three businesses. Same pattern.

They all know what good looks like. They just can't access it consistently.

Nobody is broken. Nobody lacks motivation.

They just haven't found the right thinking partner yet.

Sound familiar? What breaks your usual state?

**Engagement:** 5 likes

**What worked:**
- Took insights directly from daily messaging and turned them into a post — the field work fed the content
- Lesson: Messaging one founder a day is not just outreach. It is the raw material for posting.
- Next step: Continue to message one founder a day — every message is a potential post

**What didn't work:**
- Unsure if the post is restoring the prospect's game or just describing it — the post may be observing the problem without connecting to what breaks it
- Lesson: Start tracking which founders are liking the posts — they are signalling demand without replying
- Next step: List the founders who liked this post and check if any are prospects worth messaging

## Post 2 — March 4, 2026

**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7434890646001422336/

**Post text:**
I've been sending one message a day to a founder.

Not a pitch. Not a connection request.

Just something I noticed — in their words, not mine.

Nick wrote: "I have no system for knowing if I'm actually doing what I say I do."
He's been showing up at 5am for a year. The discipline is clearly there.
But he can't see what it's adding up to.

Stephen built his own Claude Code OS.
When someone asked about the wellbeing layer underneath it — he said "yolo."
The joke was doing a lot of work.

Jordan said: "Nothing saves us but being real."
And also: "I'm a flawed, overwhelmed human with five kids and ADHD trying to run a startup."
He's solved the external version of being real.
The internal version is still being built.

What I've noticed:

The founders who are most productive on the outside
are often the furthest from knowing what's actually going on inside.

They have tools. They have systems. They have philosophies.

What they don't have is a regulation layer —
something that connects what they're doing to how they're actually doing.

That's the gap I'm building for.

One founder at a time.

**Engagement:**
- Rana Naskar (existing prospect) replied unprompted: "Most founders can track their output. Almost none can track their state. That gap is quietly running the show."
- Field confirmation from a founder already in the prospect log — he validated the thesis from the outside, not in response to a direct message

**What worked:**
- Lesson: The thesis landed, not just the message. Rana was not in direct conversation with Shiva when he replied — he responded to the public argument. That is a different quality of signal.
- Next step: Track which prospects engage with posts. Post engagement is a demand signal, separate from direct messaging.

**What didn't work:**
- Lesson: Engagement section and learnings still being filled — only one reply observed so far.
- Next step: Check if Nick, Stephen, or Jordan (the three founders named in the post) have engaged.
